# Initial Response Toolkit Playbook

## Purpose
The Initial Response Toolkit supports the first operational period, when speed, triage, and rapid tasking matter more than detail.

## Current Repo Signals
- Placeholder panels already exist for:
  - Hasty Team Form
  - Reflex Tasking

## Primary Users
- Incident Commander
- Duty officer or dispatcher
- Operations Section
- first-arriving team leaders
- staging or check-in personnel

## Operational Goals
- establish immediate situational awareness
- launch safe first actions quickly
- capture just enough structure to avoid chaos
- document initial actions without slowing teams down
- transition cleanly into the broader incident workflow

## Candidate Tool Set
- Initial Size-Up
- Hasty Team Form
- Reflex Tasking
- Immediate Hazards
- Resource Need Snapshot
- Initial Communications Check
- Immediate Objectives
- Staging Tracker
- Quick Contact Roster
- First Operational Period Notes

## Proposed Workflow
1. Record initial report and dispatch information.
2. Capture immediate hazards and constraints.
3. Create initial objectives.
4. Launch hasty teams or reflex actions.
5. Track who is assigned, where they went, and when they are due back.
6. Convert rapid actions into formal tasking and command records.

## Home View Ideas
- current incident snapshot
- immediate hazards
- active hasty teams
- overdue check-backs
- top resource gaps
- command reminders for the next 30 to 60 minutes

## Core Records
- initial size-up record
- hasty team record
- reflex task record
- immediate hazard record
- initial objective record
- staging entry

## Important Integrations
- command objectives
- communications channels
- resource assignment
- safety and ORM
- personnel check-in
- incident timeline or activity log

## UX Priorities
- very low data-entry burden
- large, obvious status indicators
- simple timestamps
- rapid reassignment flow
- easy promotion of rough notes into formal records later

## Open Design Questions
- Is this toolkit a standalone mode or a temporary overlay that hands off into other modules?
- Which data can be intentionally rough in the first 15 minutes?
- Which alerts matter most for initial response: overdue teams, missing comms, or unaddressed hazards?
- Should the home screen prioritize actions, timeline, or status cards?
