# Disaster Response Toolkit Playbook

## Purpose
The Disaster Response Toolkit supports consequence management, damage assessment, public impact documentation, and field reporting across a broader affected area.

## Current Repo Signals
- Placeholder panels already exist for:
  - Damage Assessment
  - Urban Interview
  - Disaster Photos
- AGENTS notes that `modules/disasterresponse` is next in the MongoDB cutover queue, so this toolkit should likely align with that future module direction.

## Primary Users
- Operations Section
- Planning Section
- assessment teams
- liaison and public information staff
- recovery coordination staff

## Operational Goals
- document damage consistently
- capture field observations and interviews
- support prioritization of response and recovery actions
- connect visual evidence to locations and records
- produce a usable common operating picture

## Candidate Tool Set
- Damage Assessment
- Urban Interview
- Disaster Photos
- Area Status Board
- Infrastructure Impact Tracker
- Shelter / population impact snapshot
- Debris or access issue tracker
- Utilities disruption tracker
- Public needs and unmet needs log

## Proposed Workflow
1. Define affected areas and reporting zones.
2. Send teams to gather assessments, interviews, and photos.
3. Aggregate incoming reports by area and severity.
4. Identify priority life safety and access issues.
5. Feed validated findings into command, public information, and recovery planning.

## Home View Ideas

## UX Priorities
- location-first workflows
- attachment-friendly records
- easy field-to-command review
- consistent severity scoring
- strong filtering by area, status, and priority

## Open Design Questions
- Should photos be first-class records or attachments to assessments?
- Should interviews be separate from assessments or embedded within them?
- How should area summaries be generated: manual, assisted, or auto-aggregated?
- Which disaster workflows need standardized forms versus flexible notes?
